• 1)Start College

    There are lots of reasons that your next step should be to start college: to encourage your kids to go to college, change careers or earn a bigger paycheck, or simply to feel the sense of accomplishment that comes with that diploma. More and more jobs in the future will require a college degree of some kind. Workers who have college degrees also earn more over their lifetimes than those who don’t. So, college may be a smart next step for you. The question is: where do you start? Good news: there are a lot of resources available to you in Maine. On this site, you can explore jobs that require a college degree and are growing in Maine. You can browse or search for programs and institutions that might be of interest to you. You can find help figuring out how to pay for college, and much more.

    [accordion title="Get Help from Your Employer"]If you have a job, your first step should be to find out what supports your employer may offer to further your education and training. Many employers offer tuition assistance, flexible scheduling or on-site training to help increase employees’ skills & knowledge. If your employer is part of the Maine Employers’ Initiative, you are entitled to tuition discounts from several institutions in Maine: If your employer is on this list, ask them about it![/accordion][accordion title="The College Transitions Program"]The College Transitions Program is a great first step. Adult Education can help you explore your college options, figure out if you’re ready – and if you’re not ready, they’ll help you get ready. The Maine College Transitions Program (offered through Adult Education programs around the state) is designed to help adult students transition into college. They will provide support with applications and financial aid, college prep classes, college and career planning, placement testing, and individual advising. For a more complete description of the program: Click here. For the location of a College's Transitions program: Click here.[/accordion][accordion title="College Entrance Examination Preparation"]LearningExpress Library is offered by the Maine State Library and features over 770 practice tests, tutorials, and eBooks related to job search and workplace skills improvement, career certification and licensing exam preparation, college entrance and graduate school admissions exam preparation, GED exam preparation, and basic skills improvement in reading, writing, and math, for all ages. You will get immediate scoring, complete answer explanations, and an individualized analysis of your results. Colleges and universities in Maine have different methods of awarding credit based on each school's academic requirements.
